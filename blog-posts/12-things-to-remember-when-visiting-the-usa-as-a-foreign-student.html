12 Things to Remember When Visiting The United States as a Foreign Student

  • Home
  • Travel
  • 12 Things to Remember When Visiting The United States as a Foreign Student
  • Travel
  • 4 Aug 2018
T

oday, I’m going to share a few things that anyone who is hoping or planning to come to the United States might find useful, as mentioned earlier on my other blog and bio that I’m of African descent. I came to the U.S. in 2012 on a foreign cultural exchange program, and I’ve been here ever since. Coming to America was an exciting opportunity I ever encounter on my own. I was ecstatic to get a chance to be in America on my own as my previous travel experience has been with the family. With that being said, there was a time I’d spend long hours every day searching the net for tips, to help me survive in the USA. Therefore, I’m about to share things to remember when immigrating or visiting the United States.

Tip # 1
Get to know your American host family (this depends on your chosen program arrangement if you are going to be staying with an American family like I did)

If you’re or planning to be in the United States as a foreign exchange student, you will be placed with an American family. Try to get to know your American host family as quickly as you can. Don’t be afraid to ask as many questions, even if it means making weekly Skype arrangements with them, do it because there will be a lot to find out. Do not fly into a foreign country blindly. It's a new experience, and you’re young and going to be living with some foreign parents that are not related to you. I learned this the hard way. My first American family match didn’t work out for me. There were so many things that were amiss and some things that we neglected to discuss, which resulted in an unsuccessful match.

Tip # 2
Americans only use the Metric System

America is the only industrialized country that hasn’t adopted the brilliant use of the metric system. So, since you will be living with an American family and be attending college, you will have to learn the “standard system.” As the old saying goes, “when you’re in Rome, you do what Romans do.” You must tough this one out; no kiloliters, liters, centimeters, and kilograms here, only gallons and pounds. Don’t worry; in a few months, you will catch on.

Tip # 3
The “American dream”

The American dream is alive and well amongst the Americans and others. You might have heard, either on television or the internet, people talking about “the American dream.” I once asked my friends what is REALLY “The American Dream” while they all gave me a different definition, but the concept was quite clear. It’s simply explained as the ability to improve one’s socio-economic standard, through hard work. If you are willing to put in work and be dedicated to what you do, you too can achieve the American dream.

Tip # 4
You Must Carry Good Healthcare

If this were possible, I would have said, "Do not get sick while in America." The Healthcare insurance is important in the USA; therefore, when you’re traveling over here, make sure you are carrying an excellent health insurance. America has an advanced medical technology, and it's one of the countries that have the best doctors, but the American healthcare system is questionable and has so many loopholes. I would recommend you carry an international travel health insurance, which includes air ambulance back to your foreign country just in case something unfortunate happens while you are in the country. However, that’s just my personal advice, not a requirement.

Tip # 5
Lots Of Americans speak English and some Spanish

While some Americans are bilingual mostly by choice, the most spoken and recognized language in America is English, so you should speak English language. I hope you do because you are reading my blog, lol. In addition to that, American-English is different from the British-English. So, when you come over, regardless if English is your first language or not, you will have to learn the American-English. There’s also urban English, and the First day I heard an American “urban English,” I was shocked! Rap is a good example of that - I’m still trying to catch up on lyrics.

Tip # 6
Americans Love Their Country Flag

The U.S.A flag may seem like a standard three colored flag, but it’s an iconic and most respected flag across 50 states. Americans worship their flag, and nobody will ever disrespect their flag in their presence regardless of if you’re an American or not. Since you are preparing to come to America, be prepared to see the American flag on almost any building, either a house, car, plane, clothing, airport, souvenir shop, home game, school, or a business building.

Tip # 7
Be Sure Tip Your Server

One of the important things, especially in the service industry, which is culturally acceptable, is to show appreciation of the service by tipping your server after the service has been received. Americans are a number one tippers, and with that, they have made minimum wage jobs worthy. It is something that is installed since childhood, and the agency that I came through to America made sure to discuss. Since I’m a foreigner and couldn’t catch up on the q’s from the get-go, let’s face it. If my server was nice to me and I was feeling generous, I would invite him/her to my party instead of a tip, or give a compliment without feeling the obligation to tip.

Tip # 8
Do Not Bribe

I should probably say NEVER bribe! If you ever found yourself in a position of being pulled over by a cop in the U.S.A., do not offer money to a cop. For many people, including myself who are from countries that government workers, especially law enforcement workers, strive on receiving bribes. In America, offering a bribe to a law enforcement agent to avoid a penalty is a felony, and you will be immediately put to jail for it. If you found yourself in that unfortunate position, calm down and do as the officer ask you. Do not be a smarty pants, especially when you’re from African ancestors. If you’ve been keeping up with the international news, you are aware that there has been an uprising on the issue where cops are shooting innocent African Americans who were mistaken for posing a threat to a member of law enforcements’ safety.

Tip # 9
Stay Safe And Steer Clear of Social Media to Develop New Friendships

As exciting as it is to go to a foreign country, please and please, look after yourself. Tinder, Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, and Craigslist are not your go-to port to develop new friendships. Americans are nice people, but just like many countries, it also has its own bad apples, and most of these bad apples are lurking on the internet posing to be something they are not. So, don’t depend on the social media to develop new friendships.

Tip # 10
Help Prevent Homesickness By Following This Advice

Take into consideration that in the beginning, it will be challenging to start over in a foreign country, with no friends, your own family, and all you have is your foreign host family. There will be a point where you feel so homesick and exasperated that you will cry yourself to sleep. You would long to talk to a friend or your own family, but you really don’t want to worry them, so you will learn to tough it out; so throughout routine calls, you may find yourself telling fibs about how well you’re adapting, been there, done that. Don’t worry, you will soon have those friendships once you start with college, and it will be as easy as pie..

Tip # 11
Be Sure To Carry Your Passport and ID

In America, you are expected to always carry your personal ID. It's one of the things I didn’t get a memo of, therefore, I learned it from a personal experience. On my second week in Seattle, my fellow foreign students and I had planned to go to a dance club. All of us were from the same agency, but from different countries. Only two people in our group had carried their ID’s, and that resulted in all of us turning back home. We pretty much ruined the other’s night, so be mindful that most American nightclubs and some pubs have an allocated bouncer by the door, and before you get through the door, they will want you to show your U.S. government issued photo I.D. However, they accept all government issued passports from any country.
